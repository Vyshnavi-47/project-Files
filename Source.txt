                           <section>
                              <p>DATA TRANSFER POLICY</p>
                           </section>
                           <p>From 01/29/2020 on, we will change the data transfer policy throughout the CASM center.
                              To protect our instrument computers from potential virus issue, we request all users
                              to use our common flash drives dedicated for each instrument to copy data, then plug
                              in the data computer in 114 to copy to your own flash drive or send through Email.
                              Alternatively, you must scan your device on the data computer before plug it in any
                              instrument computer in CASM. We appreciate your cooperation and let's do our best
                              to keep a no virus environment for all users. Thanks!</p>
                           <p><a class="external" href="https://www.depts.ttu.edu/casm/includes/GEOL5304.php">Graduate students and staff who need training in TEM or SEM techniques are requested
                                 to take 5304. Techniques in Electron Microscopy and Microanalysis.</a></p>
                           <p>For help in Materials Science or reserving an SEM or TEM Microscope please contact
                              <a class="mail " href="mailto:bo.zhao@ttu.edu?subject=SEM">Dr. Bo Zhao</a>.</p>
                           <p>For Biological Science, TEM, light microscopy or Flow Cytometry contact&nbsp;<a href="mailto:%20slizarra@ttu.edu">Stephanny Lizarraga</a>.</p>
                           <p><em>To assist with designing polychromatic flow cytometry panels for use on the Cytometers,
                                 users are encouraged to visit the <a class="external" href="https://app.fluorofinder.com/ttu-casm" target="_blank" rel="noopener">FluoroFinder web-page.</a></em></p>

                           <p><strong><a class="external" href="http://sigma-aldrich.com/msds">Safety Data Sheets&nbsp;</a><br></strong></p>
                           <p>College of Arts &amp; Sciences Microscopy (CASM) of Texas Tech University located in the
                              Experimental Sciences Building ESB 112 and ESB 114.&nbsp;</p>
                           <p>The facility is open to all faculty, graduate students and area businesses interested
                              in enhancing their research using the techniques of transmission electron and scanning
                              electron microscopy. &nbsp;Trained users are given independent access to the instruments
                              they require.</p>
                           <p>The primary function of the laboratory is to train students, &nbsp;staff and faculty to
                              become proficient in the techniques required for independent research. &nbsp;In addition,
